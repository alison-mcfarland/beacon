Three Foundational Supports

Institutional Support

DDtC cannot rest on individual goodwill; wider support is vital to resource, legitimise, and embed this work—avoiding ‘decolonial washing’ (appearance of change without structural support). Planned supports include SIC funding, a community of practice, and ongoing investment. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}

Student Co-Creation

Students’ lived experiences and insights make changes more relevant, credible, and engaging; partnership also disrupts passive recipient roles. Actions include ongoing dialogue, and empowering Student Inclusivity Champions to audit modules and propose changes. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}

Educator Reflection

Colleagues’ identities and disciplinary traditions shape what/how we teach; reflecting on positionality helps surface assumptions and engage intentionally with diverse ways of knowing. Supports include reflection questions, DDtC development, and a community of practice. :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}
