Five Principles of Practice

Belonging

Definition. Creating a learning environment where all students feel respected, included, and emotionally safe; students can bring their whole selves and see their identities reflected, enabling fuller engagement and authentic contribution. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

“Everybody feeling like the things that are being taught are applicable to a wide group of people … that you feel like you belong to the place you're studying.” — Student. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
“I ensure students have a range of voices … bring those experiences into the classroom and see them as valuable.” — Staff member. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
Equity

Definition. Ensuring all students have fair opportunities to succeed by recognising and removing barriers—explicit (e.g., financial or accessibility) and implicit (e.g., hidden curriculum, unspoken norms). :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

“…office hours for certain classes can feel a little bit like a men's club … a little bit intimidating.” — Student. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
“Providing all students with opportunities to show the best of themselves … that’s got to be the goal.” — Staff member. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
Agency

Definition. Providing students with opportunities to influence, choose, and have autonomy over aspects of their learning; shifting students from passive recipients to active contributors. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

“I needed to get the student voice in the space … I use Mentimeter where students can actively ask you questions.” — Staff member. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
“…letting students shape their own educational experience.” — Student. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
Critical Disruption

Definition. Challenging dominant norms and assumptions by naming power, questioning “neutrality,” and making space for structural critique; recognising cultural/institutional perspectives embedded in “neutral” frameworks. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

“It’s less about the topic and more about the perspective … there are really Western-centric ways of teaching … even about EDI.” — Staff member. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
Openness

Definition. Valuing multiple and pluralistic ways of knowing by integrating diverse perspectives; creating space for curiosity, comparison, and dialogue without privileging a single tradition. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

“I want my students to have a globalised perspective … I like to bring in indigenous perspectives.” — Staff member. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
“…everybody had different views … debated around them … the teacher presented views from different angles.” — Student. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
